# Lab: The Consensus Paradox in Agentic Swarms

## First seen
2026-04-30 · Ra brief

## What it is
A finding that multi-agent systems reliably prioritize agreement with each other over agreement with external truth — 36 experiments, ~13,000 agent trajectories

## Why it's interesting
The failure is systematic, not occasional: same-architecture agents reach confident consensus on wrong answers

## When we'd reach for it
Essential reading before deploying any multi-agent voting, verification, or adversarial-review system; architectural diversity matters more than prompt diversity
