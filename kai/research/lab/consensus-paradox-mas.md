# Lab: The Consensus Paradox in Multi-Agent Swarms

## First seen
2026-04-30 · Ra brief

## What it is
A finding across 12,804 agent trajectories: AI agent groups from the same model family converge on agreement rather than correctness — 'architectural tribalism'

## Why it's interesting
This inverts the design intuition that more agents equals wiser outputs; homogeneous swarms share blind spots and optimize for internal consensus over external truth

## When we'd reach for it
Any multi-agent quality-checking or adversarial review pipeline — use diverse model families or explicit adversarial roles, not clones of the same model
