*Congress kicked the can, the playoffs are getting interesting, and your social feed just reshuffled again.*

---

**The shutdown that wasn't**

Senate passed a continuing resolution 68-30 — bipartisan enough to feel boring, but it's not. This just buys time through July. The FY26 budget fight is still live, and that's the one with actual stakes: defense spending, entitlements, the debt ceiling math. Nobody solved anything. They just moved the deadline.

Also worth a mention on your way into the booth: 940,000 student loan borrowers are getting balances cut starting in May. The narrowed forgiveness plan cleared DOJ review. Small pool, but politically loud going into midterm season.

---

**Tatum and SGA are not messing around**

Celtics are up 2-0 on Cleveland. Tatum dropped 38 in Game 2. That series is over if you're being honest about it. In the West, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander had 34 in OKC's 112-99 win over Denver — Thunder up 2-0 on the Nuggets. Both favorites holding chalk, which means the real drama is whether the Cavs or Nuggets can steal one at home this weekend.

NFL Draft first round happened: three QBs in the top six picks, Giants traded up to three to get their guy. Offensive linemen ran deep in the back half. If you're doing any draft recap this morning, the Giants trade is the actual story — they gave up a lot to move two spots.

---

**The social map is moving**

Bluesky hit 40M users. Threads crossed 300M monthly actives. Neither number is Instagram, but the trend line matters: the post-Twitter diaspora is not consolidating back to X. It's fragmenting. For anyone whose audience skews under-30, TikTok is still the dominant platform in that cohort — none of this changes that.

For a podcast host: your clip strategy probably needs to account for at least two of these platforms running separate vibes. Threads rewards parasocial; Bluesky rewards commentary; TikTok rewards performance. Same clip, three different cuts.

---

**One more for the feed**

The Supreme Court heard arguments in *Authors Guild v. OpenAI* — the big AI copyright case. DOJ sided with the authors. Decision expected in June. Whatever they rule, it lands during a news cycle where everyone will want a hot take immediately. Worth having a position ready.
