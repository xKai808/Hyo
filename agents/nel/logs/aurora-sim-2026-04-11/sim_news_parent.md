*A week that moved quietly — two health stories worth knowing, a record-breaking marathon, and some student-debt news that's actually real.*

**The teen weight-loss conversation just shifted.**

The FDA approved an oral version of semaglutide (the active ingredient in Ozempic) for adolescents aged 12–17 with clinical obesity. Until now the medication was injection-only; the pill form changes who can realistically access it. Insurance coverage is still an open question, and the approval covers clinical obesity — a formal medical diagnosis — not general weight management. Worth knowing it exists. Worth a conversation with your pediatrician if it ever becomes relevant to your family, rather than a strong opinion today.

**A health scare quietly closed.**

The WHO declared the most recent H5N1 bird flu outbreak over — no new human cases in 60 days across every region they were monitoring. Officials credited fast coordination in the poultry sector and a new rapid-response vaccine stockpile that was actually ready when it needed to be. Nothing to do on your end. Just good news to receive cleanly.

**Boston broke records on Monday — and the shoes are part of the story.**

Near-perfect weather, two men under 2:03, a women's winner at 2:14:50. Course records in both fields. The quieter note: carbon-plated racing shoes are now so mainstream in elite competition that commentators barely mention them anymore. If you run any distance, that technology is widely available to recreational runners and the performance difference is real. A gentle nudge if you've been thinking about new footwear for spring.

**Student debt: something is actually moving.**

A slimmed-down version of a 2024 forgiveness plan survived a DOJ review and a court challenge. About 940,000 borrowers will see loan balances reduced starting in May. If you or someone in your family has been tracking this, it's worth checking in with your loan servicer as spring arrives — disbursements are on a real timeline now.

**One for the dinner table.**

Seoul's Michelin Guide expanded to 189 starred restaurants this year, up from 136, with three new three-stars — one of them a kaiseki-Korean fusion house. Korean cuisine is having a serious global moment, and that tends to show up in home kitchens too. Worth noticing if your family has been curious about cooking in that direction.
