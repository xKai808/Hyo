## Also Moving · Governance Challenges in AI

As enterprises increasingly adopt agentic AI—autonomous systems capable of planning and executing complex workflows—they face a growing governance crisis. A recent study highlights that only 21% of enterprises have mature governance models for managing these AI agents, leading to issues like redundancy and conflicting operations.

The rapid proliferation of AI agents across business functions has created a scenario where many organizations struggle to maintain control and coherence. This lack of governance maturity can result in inefficiencies and even operational risks, as uncoordinated agents may work at cross-purposes or duplicate efforts.

Addressing this challenge requires the development of robust governance frameworks that can effectively manage the lifecycle of AI agents, from deployment to retirement. As AI continues to integrate into business operations, the need for structured governance will only become more critical.
