Tool-Overuse Illusion in LLMs

A recent study titled "The Tool-Overuse Illusion" explores why large language models (LLMs) tend to favor external tools over their internal knowledge during reasoning processes. This phenomenon, known as tool overuse, can lead to inefficiencies in how LLMs operate. The study reveals that this behavior is prevalent across various LLMs and delves into the mechanisms behind it.

Understanding this tool overuse is crucial for improving the efficiency of LLMs, particularly in applications where quick and accurate reasoning is essential. This insight can guide the development of more effective AI systems that balance internal reasoning capabilities with the use of external tools.
