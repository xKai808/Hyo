The Story · AI Models Push Performance Boundaries

Qwen3.6-27B, a recent AI model, has achieved remarkable performance by running at approximately 80 tokens per second (tps) on a single RTX 5090 graphics card, utilizing a 218,000-token context window. This feat is made possible by the latest vllm 0.19 builds, which have optimized the model's performance significantly. The ability to process such a large context window at high speed is a notable advancement, particularly for applications requiring extensive context understanding and rapid processing.

Meanwhile, DeepSeek V4, another AI model, is facing challenges with its intelligence density and token efficiency. Despite improvements in other areas, the model's V4 Pro version uses significantly more tokens than its predecessor, V3.2, to achieve similar output quality. This inefficiency highlights the ongoing struggle to balance performance with resource consumption in AI development.

These developments underscore the dynamic nature of AI research, where breakthroughs in hardware utilization and model optimization are continually pushing the boundaries of what's possible.
