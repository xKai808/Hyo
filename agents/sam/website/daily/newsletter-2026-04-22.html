## The Story · Firefox-Tor Privacy Vulnerability
A recent discovery has unveiled a privacy vulnerability in Firefox that links private Tor identities through a stable identifier. This issue arises from an IndexedDB vulnerability, which allows tracking across Tor sessions. The implications are significant, as Tor is widely used for anonymous browsing, and this vulnerability undermines its core purpose. The tech community is abuzz with discussions on how such vulnerabilities can compromise user privacy and the steps needed to address them.

Mozilla, the organization behind Firefox, is expected to respond swiftly to this issue. The timeline for a patch release will be crucial in restoring user trust and ensuring that privacy remains a top priority. This incident serves as a stark reminder of the ongoing challenges in maintaining privacy in the digital age. As we await Mozilla's response, the broader conversation about privacy in tech continues to evolve.
