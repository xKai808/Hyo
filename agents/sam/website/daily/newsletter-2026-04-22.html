## The Story · AI Scientists: Results Without Reasoning?

A recent study has put AI scientists under the microscope, revealing that these large language model (LLM)-based systems, while capable of producing results, often do so without adhering to the scientific reasoning norms that make scientific inquiry self-correcting. The study evaluated AI scientists across eight domains, including workflow execution and hypothesis-driven inquiry, through more than 25,000 agent runs. The findings suggest that while AI scientists can autonomously conduct research, their reasoning does not always align with the epistemic norms necessary for reliable scientific outcomes.

This raises important questions about the role of AI in scientific research. If AI systems are producing results without proper reasoning, it could lead to unreliable or even misleading conclusions. The study highlights the need for further research into improving AI reasoning capabilities to ensure that AI scientists can contribute meaningfully to scientific inquiry.

The implications of this are significant, especially as AI continues to play a larger role in various fields. Ensuring that AI systems can reason scientifically is crucial for their integration into research processes. As we move forward, it will be important to develop methods to enhance the reasoning capabilities of AI scientists, making them more reliable partners in research.
