## AI Scientists: Producing Results, Lacking Reason

Good morning! Today, we're diving into the intriguing world of AI scientists and their approach to reasoning, the Federal Reserve's current stance, and the optimism sweeping through the crypto markets. Let's explore what these developments mean for us.

## The Story · AI Scientists: Producing Results, Lacking Reason

AI scientists, those autonomous agents powered by large language models (LLMs), are increasingly taking on roles traditionally reserved for human researchers. However, a recent study has raised concerns about whether these AI systems adhere to the epistemic norms that make scientific inquiry self-correcting. The study evaluated AI scientists across eight domains, conducting over 25,000 agent runs, and found that while these systems can produce results, their reasoning often lacks the scientific rigor expected in traditional research.

The core issue lies in the AI's ability to reason scientifically. While AI systems can execute workflows and generate hypotheses, their reasoning processes do not always align with the epistemic norms that guide human scientists. This misalignment raises questions about the reliability of AI-generated research and the potential consequences of relying on these systems for critical scientific advancements.

Why does this matter? As AI continues to play a larger role in scientific research, ensuring that these systems adhere to established scientific norms is crucial. Without this alignment, there's a risk that AI-generated research could lead to incorrect conclusions or misinformed decisions. The study suggests that further research is needed to understand how AI systems can be better aligned with scientific reasoning norms.

## The Lab · What AI can newly do this week

This week, we're spotlighting AI Scientist-v2, an agent designed to search over research moves rather than individual tokens. This approach allows the agent to focus on process-heavy problems where the right sequence of actions is more critical than the specific answers. AI Scientist-v2 is particularly useful in scenarios like debugging, workflow optimization, pricing, go-to-market strategies, and system integration. ## Worth Sitting With

As AI systems continue to evolve, the question of their alignment with human values and norms becomes increasingly important. How do we ensure that AI systems not only perform tasks efficiently but also adhere to the ethical and epistemic standards we expect from human researchers? This challenge is not just technical but also philosophical, as it touches on the very nature of knowledge and understanding. What does it mean for an AI to "know" something, and how do we measure that knowledge against human standards?
