An email list of people who&#8217;ve opted in to hear from you is an asset that doesn&#8217;t disappear when a recommendation algorithm changes, a deal expires, or you want to try something new.</p><p>Episodes go directly to your subscribers&#8217; inboxes and can be distributed to other podcast platforms via RSS, and you can record natively&#8212;including with remote guests&#8212;using<a href="https://on.substack.com/p/introducing-the-substack-recording"> Substack Recording Studio</a>. <a href="https://on.substack.com/p/chat-guide">Chat</a> functions like a group chat for your listeners, a dedicated place for your audience to talk to each other and to you between episodes. <a href="https://on.substack.com/p/live-video-is-available-to-all-publishers">Live video</a> lets you engage, react, or connect in real time. <a href="https://on.substack.com/p/demystifying-the-feed">Notes</a>, Substack&#8217;s short-form feed, is where new listeners find you: post a clip, a quick thought, or a moment from a recent episode, and it surfaces to people who aren&#8217;t subscribed yet. Here are three models we see working well:</p><div><hr></div><h2><strong>A lightweight companion and fan community</strong></h2><p>Use your Substack publication to send episodes directly to your listeners&#8217; inboxes. To take it a step further, you can add a lightweight companion newsletter and a chat where your audience can find each other. Companion posts don&#8217;t have to be elaborate: a weekly recap, links to things mentioned on the pod, or a short note from a producer or the host can all work. In the simplest version, the chat can be set up as fan-to-fan, so you don&#8217;t have to moderate it.</p><p>This is a good starting point for podcasters who aren&#8217;t ready to launch a paid tier but want to start building an owned audience. It&#8217;s free to run, and it builds daily engagement quickly. The people who show up here are dedicated fans who are likely to convert to paying subscribers, should you choose to launch a paid tier&#8212;and now you can reach them directly whenever you need.</p><p><strong>Case study: Giggly Squad</strong></p><p><span class="mention-wrap" data-attrs="{&quot;name&quot;:&quot;Giggly Squad&quot;,&quot;id&quot;:381348397,&quot;type&quot;:&quot;user&quot;,&quot;url&quot;:null,&quot;photo_url&quot;:&quot;https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/363214fa-04ef-42c9-9d40-4045d86fc1a9_1080x1080.jpeg&quot;,&quot;uuid&quot;:&quot;5ff135f7-c8ef-439b-b2c3-27e41c1c037f&quot;}" data-component-name="MentionToDOM"></span>&#8217;s Substack is where their show lives between episodes.
