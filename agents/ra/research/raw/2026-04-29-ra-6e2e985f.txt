How&#8217;s the press tour going?</strong></p><p><strong>Lena:</strong> It&#8217;s been a ball! Last time I really did this level of public anything was the last season of <em>Girls</em> (and if you read <em>Famesick</em> you&#8217;ll see that I wasn&#8217;t really in the place to, uh, enjoy it). Since then I&#8217;ve been largely behind the scenes as a writer and director, which means you can rely on your cast to hold it down, press-wise. I have definitely pushed myself out of the little comfort zone I&#8217;ve found in recent years, but that&#8217;s because, as all the many authors of Substack know, when it comes to a book, there&#8217;s only you. What was the strategy for your Substack tour?</strong></p><p><strong>Lena:</strong> Dolly is always such an incredible sounding board, but our strategy was kind of not a strategy, in that it wasn&#8217;t super calculated. It was the result of my happily accepting the chance to collaborate with the newsletters I actually read. &#8220;Organic&#8221; is an overused word, at least in Hollywood, but I do think that when you&#8217;re a fan for real and having a genuinely great time, people can feel it. All press is not good press (another lesson of <em>Famesick</em>), and if you&#8217;re stiff, anxious, and out of place, then what&#8217;s the point? Whereas I love writing. I have a myriad of interests and hobbies and there&#8217;s a newsletter that speaks to them all. I&#8217;m also chronically ill, and so getting to show up as my best self, the self who loves to write and go down little Wiki wormholes and eBay trapdoors, is a rare treat. Writing has always made me feel like part of the world even when I can&#8217;t be, and I feel very lucky to have made new pals and learned a lot.</p><p><strong>Dolly:</strong> Lena and I first started talking in December 2024 about what it might look like for her to publish a Good Thing Going newsletter. We had been working together since 2020, and it always pained me to cut down her beautifully written Instagram captions to fit a character limit. She needed more real estate for her words. When we initially discussed a newsletter, there was some hesitation around committing to a locked cadence, but I tried to encourage her to throw out the &#8220;rules&#8221; of how to show up online. Lena is a writer. Substack made sense for her.</p><p>When it came time to promote <em>Famesick</em>, we had a large kickoff with the full team: publisher, agents, PR, management. While the PR teams focused on traditional media, I was thinking about Substack as an equally important layer of the ecosystem. Lena is the only person I know who is so offline yet deeply aware of what&#8217;s happening online. She&#8217;s an avid Substack reader, subscribes to dozens of publications, and is very engaged with her own community through DMs, Notes, and comments. So while there wasn&#8217;t a rigid plan, collaborating with publications she already read felt like a natural, grassroots way to introduce <em>Famesick</em> without it feeling too promotional. Some opportunities came through her DMs, some through my inbox, and others were friends and writers who felt like an organic fit.</p><p><strong>What were you hoping to achieve here versus other arms of the tour?</strong></p><p><strong>Lena:</strong> I wish we had a more mustache-twirling evil genius plan, but I was just &#8220;going where it&#8217;s warm,&#8221; as my father always advises. It feels like Substack folks are way more &#8220;my people&#8221; than on any other app, or on the stage of a talk show or in the pages of a magazine.</p><p>Also, as a late-in-life data nerd, it&#8217;s been really interesting to see how committed certain audiences are. I love that a newsletter with more followers but a less engaged audience doesn&#8217;t have the same value as someone with a tiny but rabid fan base. As the girl whose show regularly clocked under a million viewers (a number that, in showbiz, might as well be 10), I connect to that vibe: small but noisy, mini but mighty. It&#8217;s so cool to me that so many people are making a living as writers who wouldn&#8217;t have found a way to do that even five years ago.</p><p><strong>In January, you <a href="https://goodthinggoing.substack.com/p/hello-dolly-finally-unmasking-the">introduced Dolly</a> and revealed that you haven&#8217;t posted on Instagram directly in years.
