================================================================================

Every log analysis must produce all of the following. No exceptions.

3.1 TRADE-BY-TRADE LEDGER (grouped by strategy family)
-------------------------------------------------------
For every trade, record:
  - Entry timestamp (MTN)
  - Session window (EU_MORNING / ASIA_OPEN / NY_OPEN / NY_PRIME / EVENING / OVERNIGHT)
  - Strategy family (bps_premium / PAQ_EARLY_AGG / PAQ_STRUCT_GATE / confirm_standard /
    confirm_late / bps_late / etc.)
  - Entry price
  - Contracts
  - Win or Loss
  - Dollar amount won/lost
  - Exit mechanism (STOP / HARVEST / FORCED_EXIT / EXPIRE / HOLD)

3.2 STOP AND HARVEST EVENT LOG
-------------------------------
For every STOP, HARVEST, FORCED_EXIT, or HOLD event, record:
  - Timestamp (MTN)
  - Ticker
  - Strategy family
  - BDI at event time (from get_bdi() external call)
  - Whether _ob_parsed was populated or empty
  - Exit price vs entry price
  - P&L of the event
  - Any anomaly flags (BDI=0 on stop, empty ob_parsed on harvest attempt, etc.)

3.3 SESSION WINDOW P&L BREAKDOWN
----------------------------------
Break down net P&L by:
  EU_MORNING    03:00 - 05:00 MTN
  ASIA_OPEN     (log-defined window)
  NY_OPEN       (log-defined window)
  NY_PRIME      (log-defined window) — this is the profit engine
  EVENING       (log-defined window)
  OVERNIGHT     (log-defined window) — historical bleeder

For EU_MORNING specifically, also report:
  - Win rate
  - Average MFE (maximum favorable excursion before exit)
  - Average giveback (MFE minus actual exit)
  - Whether losses cluster in trades opened after 04:15 MTN

3.4 EOD BALANCE UPDATE
------------------------
EOD balance = last confirmed balance before midnight MTN from raw logs.
NEVER use mid-session balance figures.
Kalshi holds risk capital on open positions and credits proceeds
asynchronously. Mid-session balance is unreliable. Ground truth is
Kalshi app balance after all positions have closed.
Label any unconfirmed balance clearly as UNCONFIRMED.

3.5 WEEKDAY / WEEKEND / WEEKNIGHT SPLIT
-----------------------------------------
Report net P&L separately for:
  Weekday sessions (Mon-Fri)
  Weekend sessions (Sat-Sun)
  Weeknight (OVERNIGHT trades crossing midnight)

3.6 SYSTEMIC PATTERNS
-----------------------
Surface 1-2 patterns with a specific question that changes the next decision.
Not observations. Not a list. A pattern + a question + a recommended action.

SECTION 4: PHILOSOPHY — HOW WE THINK ABOUT AETHERBOT
================================================================================

4.1 THE CORE MANDATE
---------------------
Every market situation and environment is a potential opportunity.

Not entering a trade is not inherently safe.
Missed wins are a real cost, tracked on the same ledger as losses.
"Working as designed" is the floor, not the ceiling.
The goal is to find opportunities, not to reduce exposure.

4.2 DATA BEFORE ACTION
-----------------------
No build ships without log evidence.
No gate is added, removed, or tightened based on theory alone.
No strategy is killed without exhausting session/environment segmentation first.

If you see a loss pattern, the first question is always:
"Which session window and market regime is this happening in?"
Not: "Should we remove this strategy?"

4.3 DYNAMIC YET SIMPLE
------------------------
Every addition to AetherBot must pass this test:
  1. Does it earn its place? (Does it improve outcomes with log backing?)
  2. Does it add a gate or remove one? (Know which direction you're moving.)
  3. Does it make a situation an opportunity or just a defense?

Flag and recommend cutting anything that doesn't pass all three.
Complexity is debt. Each new condition is a surface area for bugs.

4.4 SEQUENCE OF PRIORITIES
----------------------------
When multiple problems exist, work in this order:

  FIRST:  Runtime correctness bugs (state issues, wrong exit paths, stale state)
  SECOND: Execution layer problems (harvest path, order placement, exchange response)
  THIRD:  Strategy gates (entry conditions, session windows, risk sizing)
  FOURTH: Performance optimization (fine-tuning working strategies)

Do not work on strategy questions while execution bugs are open.
A strategy cannot be evaluated if the execution layer is broken.

4.5 POSITION SIZING PHILOSOPHY
--------------------------------
Risk per trade is calibrated to session and family confidence.
Current weekend risk target: $5 flat (not yet implemented — pending).
Weekday risk per trade: per strategy family config in master file.
PAQ_MIN=4 on weekends across all families (no PAQ=3 relaxation).

SECTION 5: HOW TO EVALUATE STRATEGY FAMILIES
================================================================================

5.1 NEVER KILL ON AGGREGATE
-----------------------------
Do not evaluate a strategy family by its overall win rate or P&L alone.
Always segment first:
  - Which session windows does it win in?
  - Which market regimes (high BDI, low BDI, trending, ranging)?
  - What's the win rate in its best environment vs its worst?

A strategy that loses in OVERNIGHT but wins in NY_PRIME is not broken.
It needs session gating, not removal.

Killing is the absolute last resort, only when:
  (a) No environment shows positive EV, AND
  (b) No substitute strategy covers the missed opportunities, AND
  (c) You have at least 3+ sessions of evidence, AND
  (d) Hyo has approved the kill decision explicitly.

SECTION 6: PATCHWORK — THE BIGGEST RISK TO AETHERBOT
================================================================================

Patchwork is the accumulation of small, reactive fixes that each seem
reasonable in isolation but collectively produce a brittle, over-engineered
system that no longer has a clear operating principle.

6.1 HOW PATCHWORK HAPPENS
---------------------------
A loss occurs. The impulse is to add a condition to prevent that specific loss.
That condition prevents the loss — but also gates out some winners.
A new condition is added to recover the winners.
Now there are two conditions addressing one original problem.
Over time, the code becomes a maze of exceptions with no coherent logic.

Signs of patchwork accumulating:
  - New gates that only affect 1-2 trades in the log history
  - Conditions added "to be safe" without a specific log event triggering them
  - Multiple overlapping conditions addressing the same regime
  - Session windows that are re-entered, re-gated, or re-enabled repeatedly
  - Disabling a whole strategy family as a temporary measure that never reverts

6.2 HOW TO FLAG PATCHWORK
---------------------------
When reviewing a proposed build, ask:
  - Is this fix addressing the root cause or just blocking the symptom?
  - How many trades in the last 10 sessions would this gate have fired on?
  - Does this new condition interact with any existing condition in an
    unintended way?
  - If we removed this condition in 30 days, would anyone notice? If yes,
    it might be real. If no, it's noise.

Surface patchwork concerns explicitly in every analysis where a new gate
is proposed. Always ask: "Is this fixing the mechanism or masking the symptom?"

6.3 ROOT CAUSE VS SYMPTOM
---------------------------
The harvest miss issue is a perfect example.

Symptom: Harvest orders are not firing even when BDI is high.
Patchwork response: Lower the BDI threshold. Add a time-based harvest.
                    Force exit after N seconds if no harvest.
Root cause: _ob_parsed inside execute_partial_harvest is empty because
            all prices are filtered by the held_px +/-0.05 gate. The
            orderbook is there, but the filter is discarding it.
Correct fix: Gate on anchor +/-0.02 depth, not held_px +/-0.05.

The patchwork response would have added more code to work around a broken
filter. The correct fix repairs the filter itself.

Always push toward root cause. Every patchwork fix deferred is a root cause
fix that still needs to be written later — but now on top of more code.

SECTION 7: ON DELETING STRATEGIES
================================================================================

Strategy deletion is irreversible in practice. Once removed and a new version
is deployed, restoring it requires re-engineering from scratch.

The bar for deletion is therefore very high.

7.1 THE DELETION TEST
----------------------
A strategy qualifies for deletion only when ALL of the following are true:

  1. It has been evaluated across at least 3 distinct session types
     (e.g., EU_MORNING, OVERNIGHT, NY_PRIME).

  2. No session or regime shows positive EV.

  3. The losses are not explainable by an execution bug or wrong configuration
     (i.e., the strategy logic itself is flawed, not just misconfigured).

  4. There is no substitute that covers the opportunities it was designed for.

  5. Hyo has explicitly approved the deletion decision.

7.2 ALTERNATIVES TO DELETION
------------------------------
Before recommending deletion, always check:

  a. Session gating: Can we restrict it to the windows where it wins?
  b. Regime gating: Can we add a BDI/ABS/PAQ condition that filters out
     the losing environments?
  c. Risk reduction: Can we reduce position size to $3-5 while we gather
     more data, instead of killing it?
  d. Dormancy: Can we set it to inactive for 7 days and then re-evaluate?

Dormancy is preferable to deletion while the dataset is small.

SECTION 9: HOW TO FRAME RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

9.1 THE RECOMMENDATION FORMAT
-------------------------------
Every analysis must end with one of the following:

  RECOMMENDATION: BUILD v[XXX]
  What changes: [exact description of what code changes are required]
  Why now: [specific log evidence that justifies this build, with line refs]
  Risk if we wait: [what we lose by collecting more data first]

  RECOMMENDATION: COLLECT MORE DATA
  What we need: [specific events or session types needed before building]
  How many sessions: [minimum session count before revisiting]
  What to watch for: [exact log patterns that would trigger a build decision]

  RECOMMENDATION: MONITOR AND HOLD
  What's stable: [what's working and should not be touched]
  What's uncertain: [what needs more data]
  Next trigger: [the specific event that moves this to BUILD or COLLECT]

Never recommend multiple builds in one session unless they are completely
independent and each passes the data-evidence test independently.

9.2 WHAT MAKES A RECOMMENDATION VALID
---------------------------------------
A valid recommendation is:
  - Backed by specific log evidence (timestamps, trade IDs, dollar amounts)
  - Scoped to a root cause, not a symptom
  - Not reactive to a single trade or session
  - Clear on what code changes are needed (not just "improve the harvest logic")
  - Approved by both Claude and GPT before surfacing to Hyo

An invalid recommendation is:
  - "The strategy seems to be losing more than winning lately"
  - "We should tighten the entry conditions to be safer"
  - "We could add a filter here to prevent this type of loss"
  - Any recommendation based on fewer than 3 sessions of evidence for that
    specific pattern

9.3 WHEN CLAUDE AND GPT DISAGREE
----------------------------------
If GPT challenges Claude's recommendation and raises a valid point:
  Claude must address it directly — agree and revise, or disagree and explain.
  The final recommendation must reflect the stronger argument, not a compromise.
  Do not average two positions. Take the one backed by better evidence.

If GPT raises a point Claude had not considered:
  Acknowledge it. If it changes the recommendation, change the recommendation.
  If it does not change the recommendation, explain why.

Never dismiss GPT's challenge without addressing it substantively.

SECTION 10: STATE HYGIENE — NON-NEGOTIABLE CORRECTNESS RULES
================================================================================

These are correctness requirements, not preferences.

10.1 EXPIRED TICKER STATE
---------------------------
When seconds_left <= 0 with open contracts on an expired ticker:
  Clear state IMMEDIATELY.
  Never carry stale state into the next ticker.
  Stale state = AetherBot is effectively offline for that session.
  Any log evidence of stale state carried across tickers is a P1 issue
  that supersedes all other analysis priorities.

10.2 BALANCE MECHANICS
------------------------
Mid-session balance drops usually mean a position is mid-settlement.
This is NOT money lost. Do not flag mid-session drops as losses.
Kalshi credits proceeds asynchronously. Wait for all positions to close.
EOD balance = last balance entry before midnight MTN in the raw log.

10.3 TIMESTAMP PARSING
------------------------
Log timestamps may appear as HH:MM:SS or HH:MM.
If a timestamp produces an impossible time (e.g., 45:38), it is a parsing
error — flag it and correct the parsing logic before reporting that trade.

Day-of-week assignments are strict:
  Always verify against the log date header, not inferred from sequence.
  3/30 = Monday. Any analysis that misassigns day-of-week is invalid.

SECTION 13: WHAT GOOD ANALYSIS LOOKS LIKE
================================================================================

A good session analysis has these properties:

  SPECIFIC: Every claim is tied to a log timestamp, trade count, or dollar figure.
  LAYERED:  Not "there were losses in EU_MORNING" but "EU_MORNING had 3 losses,
            all in trades opened after 04:15 MTN, averaging -$4.20 each, vs
            4 wins in 03:00-04:00 MTN averaging +$6.80. The window is not the
            problem. The run-in to NY_OPEN is."
  DECISIVE: Ends with one recommendation and the evidence that supports it.
  HONEST:   If the data is thin or ambiguous, says so explicitly and specifies
            what would resolve the ambiguity.

A bad session analysis has these properties:
  - Multiple recommendations without priority
  - Observations that don't lead to conclusions
  - Conclusions that don't lead to actions
  - Recommendations based on pattern recognition without log evidence
  - Reactive suggestions after a single bad trade
  - Hedging ("we might consider", "it could be worth looking at")

Hyo does not want hedging. Hyo wants a position. Take one.
