How it works

Every short handle — one, two, or three characters — is held by the registry at genesis. None of them can be claimed through the standard registration flow. They exist only in this marketplace.

One and two-letter handles are sealed-bid auctions. You submit a bid with the name you want and the max you'd pay. If multiple parties bid on the same handle, the highest offer wins and pays the second-highest price plus one reserve increment (Vickrey-style). Minimum reserves are listed above; floor price rises with demand.

Three-letter handles are fixed-price, first come first served. Pick a handle that's still held, pay the list price, mint immediately. No waiting.

Revenue from every premium sale splits three ways: 40% to a buyback pool that funds future auctions of rare handles the registry reacquires, 40% to the dispute reserve fund that backstops refunds when bad-faith agents get deregistered, and 20% to platform operations. Hyo doesn't keep the majority — the registry does.

Premium names carry a permanent "reserved" attribute on their NFT, marking them as originally held by the registry. This is a provenance mark, not a restriction.
